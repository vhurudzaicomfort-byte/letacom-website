# Letacom Website — Research Findings

## Industrial Supply Leaders (Grainger, RS Components, Bidvest, Barloworld)

**Navigation patterns:** Category-first navigation with industry verticals as secondary. Grainger uses "Shop by Category" + "Industries Served" dual-nav. RS Components emphasizes product discovery with parametric filtering.

**Key takeaways for Letacom:**
- Use industry-first navigation since Letacom is a solutions partner, not a parts catalog
- Avoid deep product hierarchies — keep categories broad and inquiry-driven
- Trust signals matter: certifications, partnerships, geographic coverage

## Brewery Equipment Providers (GEA Group, Krones, Alfa Laval, Praj Industries)

**Terminology:** "Process technology", "draught dispense systems", "fermentation vessels", "CIP systems", "filling & packaging". Never "beer machines" or "drink makers".

**Positioning:** These companies present as technology partners, not equipment sellers. Solutions are framed around outcomes: "increased throughput", "reduced downtime", "quality assurance".

**Key takeaways:**
- Use proper industry terminology throughout
- Frame products within system contexts (a CO₂ regulator is part of a "draught dispense system")
- Show understanding of the full brewing value chain

## African Industrial Distributors (Bidvest Industrial, Hudaco)

**Positioning:** "Technical distribution partner", emphasis on local stockholding, technical support, and industry expertise. Multi-sector approach is common.

**Key takeaways:**
- African distributors emphasize reliability and availability over price
- After-sales support and spares availability are key differentiators
- Geographic coverage and logistics capability are trust signals

## Tier-1 African Beverage Companies (Delta Beverages, United National Breweries)

**Common procurement needs:**
- Brewing equipment: fermentation tanks, mash tuns, lautering systems
- Packaging: PET blow moulding, bottling/canning lines, labelling
- Draught: complete dispense systems, kegs, font towers, python lines, FOB detectors
- Consumables: branded glassware, coasters, packaging materials
- Infrastructure: CO₂ systems, water treatment, refrigeration
- Fleet: delivery vehicles support, branded items

## China-to-Africa Sourcing

**Positioning themes:** "Factory-direct pricing", "quality assurance with Chinese manufacturing", "end-to-end procurement". Key concern is quality verification and reliable logistics.

**Key takeaways:**
- Position as a sourcing bridge, not a reseller
- Emphasize quality control and supplier verification
- Logistics capability (China → port → inland Africa) is a differentiator
- Avoid commodity trading language — position as strategic procurement partner
